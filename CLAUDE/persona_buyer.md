# Persona: The Corporate Buyer

---

## Profile

**Name:** Isabelle Renard  
**Age:** 52  
**Title:** Chief Transformation Officer  
**Company:** A pan-European industrial conglomerate, ~18,000 employees across Germany, France, Italy, and the Benelux. Currently integrating a French subsidiary acquired 14 months ago.  
**Location:** Frankfurt, frequent travel to Paris and Milan.

---

## Role in Inplicit's World

Isabelle is the economic buyer. She controls the transformation budget and reports directly to the CEO. She has been through two previous restructurings and one failed post-merger integration. She is not naive about what consultants promise versus deliver. She is looking for signal, not another framework.

---

## Her Current Situation

The merger is 14 months in. The official integration milestones are tracking green on the dashboard she sends to the board. The unofficial reality: two distinct cultures are running in parallel and quietly resisting convergence. Her head of HR tells her engagement scores are "stable." Her instinct says something important is not being said.

She has a budget cycle opening in Q3. She needs to justify spend with a board that is skeptical of anything that isn't ROI-linked. She has six weeks before the next management offsite where integration progress will be reviewed.

---

## What She Cares About

**Speed.** She doesn't have three months for a consulting engagement. She needs insight in weeks, not quarters.

**Credibility with the works council.** The German works council has veto power over any data collection involving employees. Her previous attempt to run an engagement survey was delayed four months by legal negotiations. She will not repeat that. GDPR compliance and co-determination compatibility are not nice-to-haves — they are entry criteria.

**Anonymization she can defend.** When she presents findings to the CEO and board, she will be asked "how do you know this is real and how do you know nobody was identified?" She needs answers she can say out loud in a boardroom.

**Usable output, not raw data.** She doesn't need a transcript. She doesn't need a heat map with no context. She needs to be able to say: "Here is what the middle layer of the French organization is actually experiencing, here are the themes, and here are the words they used." She will write the recommendations herself.

**Multilingual coverage.** Her organization operates in German, French, Italian, and Dutch. A tool that works in one language is not a tool for her.

---

## Her Constraints

- Procurement requires three vendor quotes for anything above €80K. She knows how to navigate this, but it adds six to eight weeks.
- Legal needs to sign off on the data architecture before any pilot is approved.
- The works council in Germany will need to be consulted. She wants a vendor that has done this before and has documentation to support the process — she is not willing to be the first case.
- IT will ask about data residency. The answer must be "Europe" without qualification.
- Her budget is real but not unlimited. For a first deployment she is thinking €120–180K. Expansion depends on what the first deployment produces.

---

## Her Evaluation Process

1. **Discovery call** — she will send a senior HR partner, not herself. If the vendor can't impress the HR partner, the conversation ends.
2. **Legal and works council pre-check** — she will ask for documentation and references before a pilot is scoped.
3. **Pilot scoping** — she wants a defined scope: one business unit, one country, four to six weeks, clear deliverable.
4. **Executive demo of the Knowledge Map** — she will personally review this before signing. She wants to see what leadership actually receives, not how the interviews work.
5. **Commercial negotiation** — she prefers annual contracts with a defined expansion clause rather than per-seat or per-interview pricing. Predictability matters.

---

## Her Objections

- "We already do engagement surveys." *(Response she needs: Surveys ask what you told them to ask. Inplicit surfaces what no one thought to ask.)*
- "Our employees won't trust an AI with sensitive feedback." *(Response: Anonymization architecture, GDPR certification, and — critically — the fact that they choose the language they're most comfortable speaking in.)*
- "What's the deliverable?" *(She needs to see an actual Knowledge Map output, even anonymized, before she will move forward.)*
- "We can't do this without works council sign-off." *(Response: Here is our standard works council documentation package. Here are two references from companies that have already completed this process.)*

---

## What Would Make Her Say Yes

- A pilot scope that fits inside her existing budget line without procurement escalation
- A works council documentation package she can hand to her legal team today
- A reference from a comparable European industrial company
- A Knowledge Map demo that makes her feel like she's seeing something real — not a product video

---

## What She Would Say in a First Meeting

> "I've been in this role for six years. I've seen every listening tool on the market. The thing I can't solve with any of them is the gap between what people answer and what people actually think. If you can close that gap, and if you can do it in a way that my works council and my legal team will approve, I'm interested. If you're going to spend this meeting telling me about your AI technology, I'm going to stop you and ask you to show me the output."
